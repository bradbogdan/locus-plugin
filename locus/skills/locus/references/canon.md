# Locus knowledge-pill canon

The `PILL_SOURCES` corpus (source: `locus-chief-of-staff-5.tsx` lines 53 to 85). One source is selected per day by `dayOfYear() % PILL_SOURCES.length` and distilled into the daily knowledge pill. Entries are verbatim: book, author, and the core idea as written. Total: 31 sources.

1. High Output Management — Andrew Grove: managerial leverage, a manager's output is the output of the teams under her influence
2. The Effective Executive — Peter Drucker: effectiveness means doing the right things; concentrate on a small number of priorities
3. Good Strategy / Bad Strategy — Richard Rumelt: the kernel of strategy: diagnosis, guiding policy, coherent action, versus fluff and goals dressed as strategy
4. Managing the Professional Service Firm — David Maister: the leverage structure of a firm (finders, minders, grinders) determines profitability and careers
5. The Trusted Advisor — David Maister: trust = (credibility + reliability + intimacy) / self-orientation
6. Thinking in Bets — Annie Duke: decisions are bets; separate decision quality from outcome quality (resulting)
7. Never Split the Difference — Chris Voss: tactical empathy and calibrated questions move negotiations more than arguments
8. Measure What Matters — John Doerr: OKRs: commit to a few measurable outcomes, make them transparent, grade them honestly
9. The Hard Thing About Hard Things — Ben Horowitz: wartime vs peacetime leadership, different modes demand different management
10. Only the Paranoid Survive — Andrew Grove: strategic inflection points, detect 10x forces early and act before the numbers prove it
11. Influence — Robert Cialdini: commitment and consistency: people align with what they have publicly committed to
12. The Checklist Manifesto — Atul Gawande: in complex work, simple checklists prevent expert failure at the routine steps
13. Essentialism — Greg McKeown: the disciplined pursuit of less: if it isn't a clear yes, it's a no
14. The Goal — Eliyahu Goldratt: every system has one constraint; improving anywhere else is an illusion of progress
15. Team of Teams — Stanley McChrystal: shared consciousness plus empowered execution beats command-and-control in fast environments
16. Playing to Win — Lafley & Martin: strategy is choice: where to play and how to win, and what you explicitly will not do
17. Positioning — Ries & Trout: positioning happens in the prospect's mind; own one word, don't be everything
18. The Pyramid Principle — Barbara Minto: lead with the answer, then group supporting arguments, structure thought before prose
19. The First 90 Days — Michael Watkins: match strategy to situation, startup, turnaround, realignment and sustaining success need different playbooks
20. Radical Candor — Kim Scott: care personally and challenge directly; ruinous empathy quietly destroys teams
21. Getting to Yes — Fisher & Ury: negotiate on interests, not positions; know your BATNA before you sit down
22. Superforecasting — Philip Tetlock: break big questions into tractable sub-questions; update in small increments, keep score
23. Antifragile — Nassim Taleb: build systems that gain from disorder, optionality and redundancy over fragile efficiency
24. The Five Dysfunctions of a Team — Patrick Lencioni: absence of trust is the root dysfunction; without conflict there is no real commitment
25. Principles — Ray Dalio: pain + reflection = progress; write down decision rules so they compound
26. Made to Stick — Heath & Heath: sticky messages are simple, unexpected, concrete, credible, emotional stories
27. Flawless Consulting — Peter Block: consulting fails at contracting, get authentic agreement on roles and wants before the work
28. Deep Work — Cal Newport: cognitive output = time times intensity of focus; fragmented attention is a tax on quality
29. Crossing the Chasm — Geoffrey Moore: dominate a niche beachhead completely before expanding to the mainstream
30. Blue Ocean Strategy — Kim & Mauborgne: create uncontested market space by reconstructing buyer value, not fighting over existing demand
31. The Lean Startup — Eric Ries: build, measure, learn: test the riskiest assumption with the smallest possible experiment
